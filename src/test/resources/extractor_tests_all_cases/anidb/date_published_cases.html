High school student Fujiwara Takumi works as a gas station attendant during the day and a delivery boy for his father's tofu shop during late nights. Little does he know that his precise driving skills and his father's modified Toyota Sprinter AE86 Trueno make him the best amateur road racer on Mt. Akina's highway. Because of this, racing groups from all over the Gunma prefecture issue challenges to Takumi to see if he really has what it takes to be a road legend.

show review
by skates on 2017-07-10 12:45
Rating: 5.83
Approval: 82.7% (1 votes)
[TL;DR]: You'll have much more fun watching the original than watching these movies, even if you only watch the first couple seasons.

As a big fan of the original Initial D (especially the first season), this version lacks the intensity and suspense of the original. While the graphics and animation are certainly better due to being done ~15 years later, fans of the original will immediately notice that the music has become bland and uninteresting. Super Eurobeat was a key part of the original for me and it's missing completely. A lot of time is wasted in extended setup scenes that may have made sense in episode format but don't fit in a movie. The odd pacing resulted in setup scenes that dragged on and on and driving (the main point of the show!) that went by in a flash.

Story:
The pacing of the story and decision on what to include (as it's 3 movies summarizing dozens of episodes) failed to convincingly convey the transformation of Takumi from ordinary high school kid into street racer. Many times the movie would show a mostly full-length version of a setup scene (such as a race challenge or pre-race pep talk), then skimp on the important action or daily life parts that put the events into context. For example, the first race is just 7 minutes whereas in the original it lasts a full episode, yet they spent about the same amount of time on race setup. It has the feeling that rather than adapting the content to a movie length format, they simply deleted chunks until it fit the desired timescale.

Sound:
The engine sound effects were quite nice but I thought they were pretty good in the original. The OP/ED and BGM I really didn't like. It had a slow, almost meditative feel rather than the driving intensity of Super Eurobeat that felt really out of place for a show about racing. For me every time the eurobeat would crank in the original I knew things were about to get awesome. Even during the race, the music in the new version felt weak and detracted from the excitement of the race.

Character:
Similar to story, the character development is lacking due to a lot of material being forced into a small timeline.

Art/Animation:
Certainly the resolution is higher and scenes are more detailed, but to me it didn't really add anything to the actual racing. The original looks surprisingly good especially considering it was done in 1998!

Value:
If you're at all interested in a racing anime, just get the original. It's certainly longer, but you're going to miss a lot by just seeing the new version.

Enjoyment:
While with the original I couldn't stop anywhere in the first 6 episodes (seriously, the suspense is pretty incredible), I spent most of this version waiting for it to get interesting and shine on its own rather than just be a graphically updated summary of the original.
